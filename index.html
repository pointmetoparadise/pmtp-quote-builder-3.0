Setup Guide one-time setup
Complete these steps once. After that, just fill in the tool and copy the prompt for every new quote.
Part 1 — Web3Forms (sends you an email every time a client submits)
1
Go to web3forms.com in your browser. Click the big "Get Started Free" button.
2
Type your work email address and click "Get your Access Key."
Use the email where you want to receive client selections.
3
Check your email inbox. You will get a confirmation email from Web3Forms. Click the confirmation link inside it.
If you don't see it within 2 minutes, check your spam folder.
4
After confirming, Web3Forms shows you your Access Key — a string of letters and numbers like: 7b40d437-1004-48e2-9031-171a8b0ef4f1. Copy it and paste it into the Agent Info section of this builder. It never changes.
5
You can log back in at app.web3forms.com any time to see all client submissions in a dashboard.
Done with Web3Forms. One key works for all quotes and all agents at your agency.
Part 2 — GitHub (hosts your client-facing HTML quote pages for free)
1
Go to github.com. Click "Sign up" in the top right. Create a free account. Choose any username — your clients will never see it.
2
Once logged in, click the "+" icon at the top right → click "New repository."
3
Name your repository pmtp-quotes (all lowercase, no spaces). Make sure "Public" is selected. Click "Create repository."
It must be Public — Private repos cannot use free GitHub Pages hosting.
4
In the repo, click "Settings" in the top menu bar. In the left sidebar, scroll down and click "Pages."
5
Under "Branch," click the dropdown that says "None" and select "main." Leave the folder as "/ (root)." Click "Save."
6
Wait about 2–3 minutes, then refresh the Pages screen. You will see a green banner that says "Your site is live at https://[yourusername].github.io/pmtp-quotes/"
Copy this URL and paste it into the Agent Info section of this builder. Every HTML file you upload will be live at this address.
GitHub is set up. You only ever do this once — just keep uploading files to the same repo forever.
Part 3 — Uploading a Quote Page (do this for every new quote)
1
After Claude builds your HTML quote file, download it to your computer.
2
Go to github.com/[yourusername]/pmtp-quotes — your repository page.
3
Click "Add file" (top right area) → then click "Upload files."
4
Drag your HTML file into the upload box, or click "choose your files" to browse.
5
Scroll down and click the green "Commit changes" button.
6
Wait 2–3 minutes. Your quote page is live at:
https://[yourusername].github.io/pmtp-quotes/[filename].html
Example: if your file is cabo-beede-oct2026.html, the URL ends in .../cabo-beede-oct2026.html
Replacing a file: Upload a file with the same name to replace the old version. Wait 3 minutes then add ?v=2 to the end of the URL to force your browser to reload the new version.
Getting a 404 error? Wait 3 more minutes and try again. Check that the filename in the URL exactly matches what you uploaded — capital letters matter.
All quote pages live in the same repo. You never need to create a new repo.
Part 4 — Test Your Quote Page Before Sending to a Client
1
Open your live quote URL in a browser.
2
Select a hotel on each leg — the running total should update immediately.
3
Check and uncheck optional add-on items — the total should update live each time.
4
Fill in your own name and email in the form. Click "Submit My Selection."
5
The page should show a success screen. Check your inbox — a Web3Forms email should arrive within 1–2 minutes showing exactly what was selected and the estimated total.
6
Once everything checks out, send the client their PDF quote. The last page has a gold button that links directly to their selection page.
